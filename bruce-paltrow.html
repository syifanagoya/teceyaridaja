Age, Biography and Wiki

Bruce Paltrow was an American film and television producer, director, and writer. He was best known for producing the television series St. Elsewhere and for directing the films Duets and View from the Top. Paltrow was born in Brooklyn, New York, to a Jewish family. He attended the University of Iowa, where he studied theater and film. After graduating, he moved to Los Angeles and began working in the film industry. Paltrow's first major success was producing the television series St. Elsewhere, which ran from 1982 to 1988. He also directed the films Duets (2000) and View from the Top (2003). Paltrow was married to actress Blythe Danner from 1970 until his death in 2002. He had two children, actress Gwyneth Paltrow and director Jake Paltrow. At the time of his death, Paltrow had an estimated net worth of $20 million.
